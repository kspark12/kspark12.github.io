A love for Automobiles

Growing up I always had an extreme love for cars and anything that makes loud noise and goes fast. I used to scare my mom all the time as a child; when I was asked the question, "What do you want to be when you grow up?" I'd normally emphatically respond, "Moster Truck Driver," while smiling from ear to ear. Only to end up having a mother with an absolutely mortified look on her face. I can't help it, it's in my blood. With Great relatives, who either raced or owned auto shops, and a father who has always owned loud vehicles, I was destined for this life...

Car History

When I got my learners permit, My dad bought a car for us to share and have fun with. I am very blessed to have parents who support and provide for me the way that they do; just a disclaimer so I clear the air I havent paid for all of this myself haha. But it was a black out tinted, straight piped, silverstone metallic, beast of a German v8. A 2011 BMW e92 m3 competiton package. I was in love, click the link for more info on the m3. Or skip that story and click the link for the C6 corvette I bought after the m3.

My BMW m3:

I learned how to drive at speed in this car, I learned how to check oil, change fluids, understand routine maintenance, and how to truly love a car. I was opened to a world of experiences and adventures that I will never forget. I loved that car a ton and I am so lucky to have gotten to call it my 1st car, the fact my dad and I drove it together for a year and a half was amazing.

Oil leak: beginning of the end...

Altough this car was loads of fun, It had many problems. When we first bought the car we fixed the known rod bearing issue, among other things. The photo above is when I drove to my first meet with my freinds after getting my full liscense. I was so excited, but I popped the hood and saw oil everywhere. The car went on to have many issues, one of which even left me stranded 40 miles form my house. With my father and I fed up, we sold the car during a time where it had gained significant value. He went on to buy a drag built 2008 c6 z06 corvette, and I went on to buy a 2011 c6 corvette with the money we made from selling the m3. next page has info on my vete aka "Screamin_c6" on instagram.

C6 HERE...

Screamin_c6:

So in May of 2022 I bought the black 2011 base c6 corvette shown below. This car was all mine to drive, mod and enjoy. I couldn't wait. I've had multiple jobs, for the specific purpose of paying for modifications ie. parts and labor. I have since paid for a full exhaust, cold air intake a performance tune, plus plenty of cosmetic upgrades. I have many plans for the car, if I keep this specific vette, I plan to widebody it and put radials in the rear, put a massive cam, and definently upgrade some engine internals.

Spending time at Jspeed Tuning has been a blast, having a family freind be an expert LS mechanic has been amazing I'm there often jsut to hang out or see the drift vette my dad and I are having built there. I've seen my car and my dads on the dyno a feww times and it never ceases to give me goosebumps. If computers dont work out for me, I think I certainly could have a future either modding cars or flipping them for a profit.
